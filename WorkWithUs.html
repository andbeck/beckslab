PDRA (2- year)

‘Towards a general theory of ecological impacts of multiple, simultaneous stressors’. 

Applications Closed

The project involves modelling the response of complex communities (20-200 species) to multiple simultaneous forms of stress using state of the art bio-energetic food web and foraging models. Data is generated at population, community and ecosystem scales.  The primary goal is to reveal how features of stressors and features of communities combine to reveal the predictability and the generality of multiple stressor effects.

The project is based around an established, working platform for modelling multi-species communities and exploring population and structural dynamics.

PhD (ACCE-NERC)

'The stability, diversity and complexity of food web networks under climate change'

We are taking applications for a NERC funded PhD through the ACCE Doctoral Training Programme. (UK/EU Students Applicants Only)

The structure and complexity of food web networks underpin our understanding of biodiversity and the stability, persistence and functioning of ecological communities. Furthermore, communities are facing multiple, simultaneous threats from climate change. There is a pressing need to develop predictive tools that help mitigate the impacts of climate change on large terrestrial, marine and freshwater communities. 

Key questions to be considered include

(1) can we develop models to better predict food web stability, diversity and complexity when there are species that eat thing bigger, rather than smaller than them (like parasites)? 

(2) can we conduct computer-based experiments to predict how multiple, simultaneous aspects of climate change influence the stability, diversity and complexity of communities? 

(3) can we reconcile food web models that focus on species with other types of models that focus on size? 

The project suits a student with a background in ecology and conservation and an interest in developing quantitiative skills (simulation and data driven modelling).

PhD (Sheffield)

'The coherence of stability across ecological scales under multiple stressors'

We are taking applications for a University of Sheffield funded PhD studentship. (UK/EU/Interational Applicants)

A fundamental property of populations, communities and ecosystems is their stability. Functionally stable ecosystems provide clean water, food and maintain biodiversity.  There is a pressing need to make predictions about the coherence of stability among ecosystems facing multiple, simultaneous stressors tied to climate and environmental change.

Key questions to be considered include

(1) how does foraging biology affect robustness to extinction in modern, historical or even ancient communities of species?

(2) How do we robustly challenge predictions about dynamics and stability using real data? 

(3) How does stability of populations relate to stability of biodiversity and ecosystem function?

The project suits a student with a background in ecology and conservation and an interest or skills in developing quantitiative skills (simulation and data driven modelling).
